title: Avoiding Bans
description: How to avoid WhatsApp number bans — ban types, causes, and protection methods
---

Having your WhatsApp number banned is one of the most serious issues for a business. Recovery can take from several days to several weeks, and in some cases the number is permanently blocked. This section covers ban types, their causes, and protection methods in detail.

## How to check your number status

Regularly check your number status in WhatsApp Manager:

1. Go to WhatsApp Manager in Meta Business Suite
2. Open the **Phone Numbers** section
3. Check:
   - **Number status** — should be "Connected"
   - **Quality rating** — Green, Yellow, or Red
   - **Messaging limit** — current limit on unique contacts

<Callout type="warn" title="Monitor your rating">
  If the quality rating has dropped to yellow — this is a warning signal. Immediately analyze what is causing complaints and adjust your broadcast strategy. A red rating can lead to reduced limits or a ban.
</Callout>

## Quality rating

Meta evaluates your number quality based on feedback from message recipients:

- **Green (High)** — excellent quality, few complaints
- **Yellow (Medium)** — moderate number of complaints, needs attention
- **Red (Low)** — many complaints, limits may be reduced, ban risk

### What affects the rating

- Number of users who tap "Block" or "Report" on your messages
- Frequency of sending messages to users who do not reply
- Quality of template message content

## Ban types

### 1. Inappropriate Website

**Causes:**
- The website listed in the WhatsApp profile or in message templates does not comply with Meta's policy
- The website contains prohibited content (gambling, adult content, counterfeit goods)
- The website is down (404) or leads to a suspicious domain
- URLs in messages use link shorteners that Meta cannot verify

**How to fix:**
1. Check all links in your profile and message templates
2. Make sure the website is working and contains legitimate content
3. Replace shortened links with full URLs on your domain
4. Submit an appeal to Meta Business Support with screenshots of the website

**How to prevent:**
- Use only links to your own domain
- Do not use link shortening services (bit.ly, tinyurl, etc.)
- Regularly verify that all links in templates are working
- Make sure your website has terms of service and a privacy policy

### 2. Scam

**Causes:**
- Sending messages with promises of unrealistic benefits
- Requesting personal data or payment information in messages
- Impersonating another business or government agency
- Mass user complaints about fraudulent activity

**How to fix:**
1. This is the most serious type of ban — recovery is extremely difficult
2. Submit an appeal with a detailed description of your business
3. Provide documents confirming the legitimacy of your company
4. Be prepared for a lengthy review process (up to 30 days)

**How to prevent:**
- Never request passwords, PIN codes, or card details in messages
- Do not promise guaranteed income or unrealistic discounts
- Clearly state the company name in every message
- Complete Meta verification to confirm legitimacy

### 3. Spam

**Causes:**
- Mass sending messages to users without their consent
- Sending a large number of messages in a short period
- High percentage of users blocking your messages
- Sending identical messages to a large number of contacts

**How to fix:**
1. Pause all broadcasts for at least 24 hours
2. Analyze which messages caused complaints
3. Submit an appeal with an explanation and improvement plan
4. After recovery — start with small volumes and gradually increase

**How to prevent:**
- Send messages **only to those who have given consent**
- Add an **"Unsubscribe"** button to every template message
- Start broadcasts with small volumes (50–100 contacts) and gradually increase
- Do not exceed 1,000 messages per hour for a new number
- Segment your audience — send relevant messages to each segment
- Maintain pauses between broadcasts (at least 24 hours)

### 4. Commerce Policy Violation

**Causes:**
- Selling goods prohibited by Meta's policy (weapons, drugs, tobacco, alcohol)
- Selling counterfeit goods or violating copyrights
- Misleading descriptions of goods or services
- Lack of clear information about prices and terms

**How to fix:**
1. Remove all templates related to prohibited goods
2. Update your product catalog, removing violating items
3. Submit an appeal confirming the corrections
4. Provide licenses and certificates if necessary

**How to prevent:**
- Review the Meta Commerce Policy before starting
- Do not promote products from prohibited categories
- List real prices and delivery conditions
- Use actual product photos, not stock images

## General protection recommendations

### Number warm-up

A new number must be "warmed up" before mass broadcasts:

1. **Days 1–7**: Only personal conversations, replies to incoming messages
2. **Days 7–14**: Small broadcasts — up to 50 contacts per day
3. **Days 14–30**: Gradual increase to 200–500 contacts per day
4. **After 30 days**: Full-scale broadcasts within Meta's limits

### Unsubscribe button

Add an option to unsubscribe to every broadcast template. This can be implemented as:

- A quick reply button "Unsubscribe from mailings"
- Text at the end of the message: "Reply STOP to unsubscribe"

<Callout title="Important">
  A user who taps "Unsubscribe" must be **immediately** removed from all broadcasts. Continuing to send messages after an unsubscribe is a direct path to a ban.
</Callout>

### What to do with a yellow rating

1. Pause all active broadcasts
2. Analyze recent templates — which ones caused more complaints
3. Review your contact list — remove inactive numbers
4. Reduce broadcast frequency
5. Improve content — make messages more personalized and useful
6. Wait 7 days — the rating updates weekly

### Monitoring in Pleep

Pleep provides monitoring tools:

- **Integration dashboard** — current connection status and quality rating
- **Broadcast statistics** — delivery, read, and complaint rates
- **Notifications** — automatic warnings when the rating drops
