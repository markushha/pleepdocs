Templates are pre-built messages used for WABA broadcasts via the WhatsApp Business API. Every template must be approved by Meta before it can be used.

## Why are templates required?

WhatsApp requires that all outbound business messages (except replies within a 24-hour window) use approved templates. This protects users from spam and ensures communication quality.

<Callout title="When are templates needed?">
  Templates are required for **WABA Broadcast** and **WABA Coexistence Broadcast**. Regular WhatsApp Broadcasts do not require templates.
</Callout>

## Template categories

Meta divides templates into three categories:

- **Marketing** — promotions, discounts, special offers, new product announcements. Used for customer acquisition and retention
- **Utility** — order notifications, appointment confirmations, status updates. Related to a specific customer action
- **Authentication** — one-time verification codes and passwords

<Callout title="Please note">
  The template category affects the cost of sending. Marketing templates are generally more expensive than utility templates.
</Callout>

## How to create a template in Pleep

1. Go to **Broadcasts** → **Templates**
2. Click **Create Template**
3. Enter the template name (Latin characters, no spaces, e.g. `order_confirmation`)
4. Select a category: marketing, utility, or authentication
5. Choose the template language
6. Write the message text, adding variables if needed (`{{1}}`, `{{2}}`)
7. Add buttons or quick replies if desired
8. Submit the template for review

## Tips for successful approval

- **Be specific** — clearly describe the purpose of the message. Avoid vague wording
- **Do not use shortened links** — Meta may reject templates with bit.ly and similar services
- **Use variables correctly** — a variable should not make up the majority of the message
- **Follow Meta policies** — do not send content that violates WhatsApp Business rules
- **Provide examples** — when creating the template, include sample values for variables

## Review timeline

Meta typically reviews templates within a few minutes, but in some cases the review may take up to 24 hours. You will receive a notification when the template is approved or rejected.

<Callout title="Template rejected?">
  If your template is rejected, check the rejection reason, make adjustments, and resubmit. Common reasons include: overly generic text, missing context, or Meta policy violations.
</Callout>
